# Wurkz — Positioning & Pitch Language

> Owner decision 2026-07-09: the product is **Wurkz**. The desktop app is **"Wurkz Shop"**;
> the cloud app is **"Wurkz Cloud"** (spoken: *"the Owner's Dashboard"*). "Zorviz" survives only
> in code internals (packages, DB filename, crate names) until a deliberate low-risk rename —
> branding-level renames (UI strings, installer, docs, sales material) use Wurkz.

## The two products, in the customer's words

### Wurkz Shop (the desktop app)

**One-liner:** Your shop's cashier, logbook, and filing cabinet in one.

**The pitch:** Job orders, estimates, inventory, cash drawer, receipts — all on your own
computer. It works even with no internet, and your records never leave your shop.

**The load-bearing phrase:** *"works even with no internet."* For a PH shop with a spotty
connection this is not a feature — it's the difference between usable and not.

### Wurkz Cloud (the Owner's Dashboard)

**One-liner:** How you watch your shop from anywhere.

**The pitch:** Your phone at home, another city, abroad — sales today, who owes you, who you
owe, whether the cash drawer balanced, and whether anything looks off. Without calling the
shop or driving there.

### The pair, together

> **"Wurkz Shop runs your shop. Wurkz Cloud lets you *watch* it — from anywhere."**

**The money line:** Wurkz Shop is bought once (licensed); Wurkz Cloud is the monthly
subscription. *"The shop pays for itself; the peace of mind is ₱X a month."*

## Feature → benefit, per audience

**To the hands-on owner (in the shop daily):** speed and paperwork. Estimates that print
professionally, receipts in one tap, inventory that warns before you run out, the BIR
Senior/PWD record kept automatically.

**To the absentee owner (the real Cloud buyer):** trust and visibility. Drawer variance by
who closed it, discounts by who gave them, aging balances, the weekly digest. The hook:
*"you'll know — without being there."*

**To the multi-shop owner (the best customer):** one login, all branches side by side.

## Naming rules

- First mention in any material: **Wurkz Shop** and **Wurkz Cloud**. After that, "the Shop
  app" / "the Cloud" is fine.
- "Owner's Dashboard" is a *description* of Wurkz Cloud in speech, not a product name — don't
  print it as a title.
- Casual/verbal: just **"Wurkz"** refers to the whole thing.
